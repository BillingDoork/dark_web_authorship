Post by: Chip Douglas on May 12, 2013, 04:16 am
NateRiver - It happens from time to time. So there's always that 'outside chance' that the the disappointment you felt when your regular delivery came and went w/o 'the goods',  will be made up for by that one odd day, where they have your expected order on the later truck. A family member was a lifer at the USPS.

Anyway, as said before, 'many variables' go into the decision to send out certain parcels on "the late truck". - Could be the transfer station that your zip code's packages are sorted through, didn't get the delivery from the next nearest major hub, until after the other truck left, that takes your areas packages to your local PO.

Then again it could be some sort of Holiday. Maybe one you never considered? Maybe Mothers Day?- -So many scenarios. I think I don't have to list more.
It's been happening that way for a while, you just never took notice. Most of us suddenly become 'hyper-aware' of the USPS's delivery habits, when we take that giant entrance ramp onto The Silk Road!  ;)

Suffice it to say. You're going to see all sorts of delivery methods, now that you're 'tuned in' to the whole process.

Last summer I started to really become a regular buyer on here, and was either pissed that the "late truck" stopped coming, or paranoid that "Hey, they never come at 5 O'clock" ???

Hasn't happened since then, but from what I've learned, it happens more in the summer for whatever reason. Also the ever slowing economy has caused cutbacks. Even Saturday delivery will end in the US in August of this year. They say Priority, Express, and even parcel post packages will still be delivered "in some areas", but regular mail, and office hours on Saturday, will end.  :-\
