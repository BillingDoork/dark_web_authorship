They don't open soldiers mail, except in boot camp if it's a package.  It's usually a care package that the DI's eat right in front of you while they make you do pushups in the mud.

They don't read outgoing/incoming mail either... it's not prison.  What they will do is execute you for treason if you write letters home talking about operations and shit.  (after due process, but in real, active war time, I mean... accidents do happen)   In WWII, there was a man executed for deserting.  No trial, they just executed via firing squad.  The point being, rules in the military are not that of a civilian.

It is true that mail in the military is subject to military law, as in, warrant-less searches.

IMO - I think you'd be nuts to have drugs sent to your APO-FPO address.  But hey man, best of luck!
