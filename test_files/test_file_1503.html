Title: Re: Willing to pay for a method to ship WITH tracking.
Post by: MissNatural on July 28, 2013, 06:27 am
How do they do it? I will tell you how they do it.

They order online the postage online from USPS or a related website with real information/real payment information, though probably not of their own, at least it would be dumb to use their own. All they'd have to do is use someone's information who doesn't dabble in any illegal activity, and obviously this someone would have to consent to an extent. If police ever track the payment back they will run into this person who doesn't do anything illegal and all a vendor can do is put their trust in this person.

Little do many people know you can order tracking labels for free on the USPS website and stamps.com, though since they are free they literally take forever to get to you. I actually had orders placed for 1000 tracking labels (5 orders of 200 to different houses), and none of them ever shipped out or arrived. I even used real information on some of them.

These are just 2 ways to do it. Personally I believe a lot of vendors take a huge risk and use their real information with tracking. Honestly I don't feel it to be that much of a risk... if LE are going to bust you through that manner it would require a lot of effort and resources, as well as multi-department co-operation, and even further co-operation with the USPS website management, and/or the management of paypal, all of which may yield no results at all or lead to a person who had their credit card stolen, etc.

There are ways to do it, but honestly it's not worth the hassle. For most vendors 99/100 orders arrive perfectly fine, at least with domestic orders. From my experience, and talked with a few domestic only USA vendors, very few ever even have a need for tracking as all their packages arrive.

Title: Re: Willing to pay for a method to ship WITH tracking.
Post by: PowerToCharm on July 28, 2013, 01:27 pm
You can absolutely print a label with tracking and no postage using USPS Shipping Assistant, but another poster makes an excellent point, which is that buyer's names and addresses are entered (and almost certainly saved) in the USPS system. This seems like a disservice to buyers, though the vendor would be protected because financial info is not required with Shipping Assistant. I could find no tracking labels to order on the USPS Web site, only labels for certified mail, registered mail, signature confirmation, etc.

It seems to come down to walking into the post office and paying for postage and tracking across the counter, using cash of course, and just taking your chances with whatever cameras might be in operation. This could be inconvenient if you're sending out more than a few items a week, because going to the post office takes time, especially if you feel the need to go to several different ones. Also, tracking costs 90 cents at the post office, and it's absolutely free if you use online postage. Over the course of a year, 90 cents per item could make a significant difference in the bottom line for a busy vendor.
