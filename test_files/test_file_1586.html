Title: Re: big order, got usps letter asking to sign
Post by: MissNatural on August 11, 2013, 11:26 pm
You should be fine to sign. A lot of times with international orders even if the sender checks no signature required, the receiving country may require it.

Lets discuss a few things. Now adays it seems they don't even need you to sign for it. If you live in a residential area, they can just put it on your doorstep, and bust in your house a minute after you bring it inside, IF they are watching you. If they were going to get you to sign for it before raiding you, they would do it at your doorstep, and reattampt delivery most likely. They would not allow you to come get it on your own terms and time convienence. LE doesn't work like that. One of the biggest tips for avoiding LE stings is you set the time, place, etc. LE doesn't always have time to sit around all day and wait for you.

Also remember, if you don't seem like a total sketch-ball, there is always a TON of plausible deniability for a lawyer to get the controlled delivery/conspiracy charge dropped in court. They mostly use such deliveries to get inside your house and find other drugs, and charge you with those. Now don't think you still won't get charged for a delivered package... it all comes down to how you play your cards.

In your case, I would assume you are fine to get your package.

Post by: SilkRoadRambler on August 12, 2013, 01:31 am
Sounds like your normal international delivery attempt. Most parcels from overseas that have tracking require a signature. If you want, instead of picking it up you can sign the back of the peach 3849 slip and check off either "redelivery" at a time you're there or you can request to have it left in your mailbox/at your door by signing for it without seeing it. Anyway you do it, I'm sure everything is fine.
