Title: USPS Media Mail: Subject to Inspection
Post by: PowerToCharm on July 27, 2013, 12:06 pm
I'm sure everybody knows this, right? But just in case... Media Mail can be opened for inspection by any postal employee at any point in its journey- no warrant or Postal Inspector General or other hoop-de-doo such as would be required to open a piece of First Class or Priority Mail.

Media Mail is for media only- books and such- and its often way cheaper than other mail classes. As a result, people try to use it to send non-media items and save on postage, so the postal service has given itself permission to inspect MM at will to make sure they only contain allowable media items.

Subject to Inspection! Thank you and good day.
