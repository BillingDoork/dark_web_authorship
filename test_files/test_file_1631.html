Define risky? there is a definite chance of being caught, more so than on the Mexican border, and there is also a good chance of not getting caught.  Now, I've never brought weed over, but I've brought other stuff, and I've known smugglers.  You chances of being caught, assuming your vehicle, story, background, and stay in canada are legit, is about 1-5% depending on who is working.  If a dog sniffs your vehicle, you have a decent chance of being caught.   They second-stage(dogs, etc.) about 1 out of 20 vehicles, so that is where I came up with the 5% number.  Now, land crossing is definitely an option, a remote place where roads lead from canada to the the US would be idea, cross on foot and have a vehicle pick you up.  The locals use 4-wheelers and snomobiles, so going stealth is the way to go.   Another thing you can do, is do a dead drop just over the border, up in a tree and use a GPS to geocache it.  Then, come back later after returning to canada.  THe people who live along the border fall into two groups, those who hate smugglers, and those who like/are smugglers(but not you) either way, don't count on the locals helping you at all, they will call CBP in 2sec and most of them have dogs which bark when you get near the border.  The only real foolproof way, is to go through the maine wilderness and canoe/kayak through 75km of swam..
