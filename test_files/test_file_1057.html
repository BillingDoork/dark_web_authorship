We just had one sent USPS Priority that took 6 days to arrive. It was delivered just fine and was totally straight, but after examining the tracking (we don't check unless asked) it had bounced around two different WRONG cities before ending up in the right place. We also had a second package take 5 days, going to one wrong city. We have spoken with USPS on a few different levels (Carrier, Store Clerk, Manager on Phone) and all have echoed the same idea that they are just a bit behind on their day-to-day routine and things are occasionally getting backed up. We were also told that many of the carriers currently are "stand-ins" that were not trained to the same level a new hire by the USPS would be. This is obviously all a result of the budget cuts. We see this knowledge as meaning two things. 1) If your package isn't quite on schedule it is not outside the normal. Tracking also seems to be a bit on the fritz. 2) The postal workers all seem to be very overloaded, we HYPOTHESIS (NOT A FACT) the idea that many of the workers are not trained as meaning that perhaps more packages will get through than typically would, BUT ALSO there may be errors in the mail system that wouldn't usually happen. We think that these risks can largely be avoided by using MBB's and having your labels printed so there is no room for error in reading bad handwriting. All in all we think it's best to avoid panic over packages taking a day or two longer than you might expect. Ebay and Paypal shipping forums echo this sentiment.
