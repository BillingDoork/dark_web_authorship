I haven't ordered anything off SR - yet.  If the packing is done right, and the address is correct the only way it will be intercepted is with bad luck.
Nobody should be sending anything that requires a signature.  If  something is sent Express Mail sender should waiver the signature-that way the mailman just drops it off and goes on.
If the package wont fit in a mail box most drop it off at the door and move on. But in some neighborhoods of high crime, or for what ever other reasons dog on porch-Gate locked etc etc, they will leave a notice to pick up the package at the P.O. ,
and that is when you will get nervous and think it might be a setup. It's just a notice to pick up your package, if it doesn't require a signature and you used a fake name they really shouldn't ask for an ID but might anyway and so things are screwed up now so,
for that reason you might use your real name along with the fake name...example....

Mr Fake Name
Care/of Mr Real name
your address
Your city State Zip

The bad thing about fake names is....if you are popped you are going to have to make up some fairy tail, about your imaginary friend.

Do NOT use the name of an old resident. If the mailman knows they moved he will forward the package -if they put in a forward -or he will send it back it they didn't put in a forwarding address, as "Moved Left no Address" If they moved long ago, it will get sent back as, "Forward order expired".

Really the best options are.....

1. Your name and your address and hope you are not a part of the 1% -if you are keep your mouth shut and make them prove in court any charges-it wont be easy.
2. A friends name and friends address  (hard to find friends like these)
