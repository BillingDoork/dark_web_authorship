Title: Established vendor, terrible shipping
Post by: qokepow on November 29, 2012, 11:51 am
I've been having an issue with a specific vendor I've used several times, and wondered what others opinions might be.

I've had 4 successful transactions with them already - the product is generally good, the packing is mostly OK, but the shipping times have been poor. All transactions were paid for as next day delivery and then took 5 - 7 days to arrive after being marked as "in-transit".

The vendors reason for this, which I've seen them remark to other buyers is "blame royal mail". I'm generally OK to wait a while for my shipments, but not when it's consistently the same delay and I've paid for a quick service and told the products have been dispatched.

Anyone in the UK should agree that something posted 1st class should almost always arrive the next day, and take 2 days at the absolute most.

The vendors profile page states (reworded slightly to avoid pointing them out):

"We dispatch all shipments first class the same day and they will arrive the next day".

Being the good natured buyer I am, I've still always given them a 5 star rating, albeit with a comment about slow delivery times.

This is where things get interesting.

On Tuesday I sent them a message saying I was thinking about making another purchase, but only if they could guarantee delivery by Friday. This should be 100% possible if the item were sent that day or even the next day. I was reasonable and civil in my message and outlined my previous experience. I said it was OK if they couldn't make this promise, but that in that case I would not be buying from them anymore and might have to reconsider my ratings of them.

Today I received a message from them which essentially confirms my suspicions they've been talking nonsense and are almost certainly only bulk dispatching once or twice a week and not every day as promised.

They said they were sorry for my delay, but it was because there was a postage strike (there definitely isn't). They said my package was in the depot and  I should definitely have it tomorrow and that they'd included an extra to make up for it.

Given I haven't actually made another order, it's clear they are just giving out cut/paste answers about postage delays which are total bullshit, and aren't even bothering to read customer messages properly.

So now I'm considering dropping all my previous ratings for them from 5 to 4 (or even 3 for one that took ages).

Does this seem like a reasonable thing to do?

Also, the bigger question I have is, should we feel so obliged to be giving all vendors a 5 star rating regardless of postage problems. Almost all vendors, especially this one, have a message to the effect of "GIVE US A 5 STAR RATING OR YOU WILL NEVER BUY FROM US AGAIN". This seems like a complete imbalance in favour of the vendors, and skews the real ratings they deserve.

Ratings should accurately reflect the service and product received. It seems like at the time, you thought they'd earned 5s, but now in hindsight, you think a more accurate appraisal is 4s or 3s. If a vendor is telling you nonsense in PM, that's definitely bad communication and deserves points off. If they have a "5s only" policy but won't actually act like a 5-star vendor, don't buy from them again until they shape up.
