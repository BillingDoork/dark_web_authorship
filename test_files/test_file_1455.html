There is nothing too unusual about the tracking history of the package as you've described.  Obviously, there some issue preventing it from being delivered to you but there's no real way to know at this point other than what tracking tells you.  It's doubtful the pkg has been seized.

Here's what you CAN do to resolve things providing you are willing to make inquiries.  There are any number of legitimate reasons why it's not been delivered yet.  Find the local phone number of the USPS faculty that last scanned the pkg...this is most likely a sort facility.  Do this by goggling the zip code listed on that last known scan.  Call that facility and ask for the Supervisor on duty and make your inquiry with that person using the tracking number.  If that's where it was last scanned that's where it most likely sits.  You may be able to find out why it's not been delivered and make any corrections necessary..or you may be able to go physically pick t up providing you have ID that matches the addressee.
