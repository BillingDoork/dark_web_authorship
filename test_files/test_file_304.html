Title: Re: reg. letter in customs for 4days but never inbound please advice vets w/similar
Post by: Snappy Tom on October 26, 2012, 01:30 pm
Registered letters can be and usually are VERY SLOW.  This is because registered mail must be signed for at every single step. When it changes hands (custody) at ANY point, it must be accounted for and signed off on by the new custodian. Such as from you to the post office, postal worker #1 -> postal worker #2, the post office to the airport package loaders, from the airport mail handler -> customs agent #1, from customs back to the destination country post office sort center, from the sorting center to the next sorting center, from there to the delivery truck driver... every one of those hand offs has to be signed for. So it can take forever.

I once had a registered mail piece come from Europe to the USA. What normally takes 6 days via standard air mail took more than 2 months via Registered Mail. But it was not opened by anyone. It just took a long, long time.

Sometimes stuff coming into the country will say inbound into customs and outbound from customs, but sometimes it will not say that. I'm not sure what determines if it says so or not, however I'm pretty sure all mail of every sort goes through customs.
