In my opinion as a buyer, if the vendor doesn't offer a DCN, then you both share in the responsibility if something goes wrong.  Neither of you can prove who is at fault.  If you're in resolution and that is the way it is going, then you should offer a settlement of 50% loss. As for the international order, you may want to extend the date and be nice to the vendor.  It could just be delayed.  I don't know what you ordered, but it could have been seized.

Without a DCN who is to say that anything was ever shipped? Makes no sense to me.  It's a layer of protection for all.

Little late now, but if you are a new buyer, you really, really should stick to smaller domestic orders.  Build some credibility and stay in a safe zone in case of trouble.  Once you have that relationship with a vendor(s) you can proceed to take more risk.
