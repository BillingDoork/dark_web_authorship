Here's the thing OP,

In so far as my puny brain understands it, Express packages are well known for and have been known for a long time now as the preferred method of USPS drug shipment. There are various posts in the stickies and all over this forum explaining that Postal Inspectors, the guys you really have to watch, know good and well that if they are handling a package that smells like grass and that package is an express package that they've got a good case for trying to get a warrant to open that package.

If anyone remembers the baseball player that got arrested about a month ago, he received, I believe two pounds of marijuana in either express or priority packages.

The USPS knows that because of the extreme time limits that Express packages are under, they "in theory" should be subject to less scrutiny in order to make delivery deadlines. At least, this is what the USPS believes your common drug mailer is thinking. So they use that fact to give Express packages greater scrutiny.  The fact that your regular, street-beat mailman knows that Express packages are occasionally used for drug shipments should tell us that people within USPS, not just Postal Inspectors, are educated to look for certain things. Things like Express packages.

Just be careful man. That's all it comes down to. There is no high, no drug, no pharmakeia in the world worth sitting in a cell for. Just relax. Next time you order, order more than you need right now. It is hard at first. I remember when I got on here I felt like an overweight woman at Golden Corral. I wanted to try it all. After a while though the novelty wears off and this place, more or less, because another market that you transact at.

Take care my friend and may the Fourth be with you.
