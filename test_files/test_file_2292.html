Blunts, you're lucky in that it was not a controlled delivery. Or it doesn't appear to have been, at least. In a controlled delivery, an under cover postal inspector(mail-cop) will ask you to sign for the package, demand to see your ID before letting you sign(may be phrased as a gentle request), accept your signature which counts as proof that you have now received illegal drugs, then they will sit back for an amount of time, which may be quite variable, to give you enough time to open your package. After this variable amount of time, they will serve up a search warrant for your location, as they are now certain you are knowingly holding illegal drugs on the premises. This is, as far as I've read, typically done within an hour to negate any chance of the drugs being disposed of or secretly passed off to someone else.

So what you should do is contact the vendor immediately and get him to tell you EVERYTHING about why he had to sign for it. If the vendor sent it without a signature required, you need to flush your everything illegal/suspicious down the drain as soon as you can.

The reason a controlled delivery can occur is a package may have gotten flagged as suspicious, which results in it being set aside to get some attention from a drug dog. Should the dog get too friendly with the package, LE gets a warrant to inspect the package on the inside, which they document in exacting detail, to find if there are any drugs. In the event of discovering drugs, they may or may not remove a portion(depending on drug-value and amount) then continue with the delivery via the above controlled delivery method. Special warning; IF SOMETHING SMELLS LIKE DRUGS, LE WILL JUST GET A WARRANT TO SEARCH THE DESTINATION-ADDRESS.
