Post by: jagfug on December 28, 2012, 11:32 am
Another good idea, and I found this out from being a big ebay seller.

Always include the +4 Zip Code. If you don't know it, go to USPS.com and click-look up zip codes. - Put in your address, just leave out your zip. It will return with your address, they way they want it to look, and it's always all caps, so I don't get these vendors saying, "Orders with addresses in ALL CAPS will be cancelled. Usually overseas, if you're a US vendor, and you don't know that, you should.

That zipcode +4 - saves it from one extra step of being "hand sorted". One less pair of hands? Sounds worth it to me!

Ever see a computer generated sticker under your address on a letter? Usually on like a birthday card that has a hand written address. Because it doesn't have the +4, it gets rejected by the scanner on the

conveyor belt, and lands in a big bin. Then it sits there waiting for someone to come back from their 4th mandated coffee break of the day (Unions???) They then take each parcel/letter, type in the address,

and it gets that sticker with the +4 zip on it.

All of that could have been avoided if the +4 were on there already.
