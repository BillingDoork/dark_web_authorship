Title: Re: Will too many international orders raise eyebrows? [USPS specific]
Post by: oxyhoot on September 16, 2012, 08:15 am
I agree with others on this. I have a business and a postal box to ship to so I never have to sign, and I have the added bonus that I can received stuff from certain countries and it doesn't look out of place or suspicious, unless the item is packaged poorly but especially when ordering internationally, I have been keeping it to one certain vendor who's packing methods rock the house, has good prices and products and his stuff comes almost like clockwork. 9-10 working days almost everytime. Never been disappointed by them.

Now if I lived on a rural route, and had stuff shipped internationally to my name, not my business name, I wouldn't order much, if anything at all. In my opinion unless the packaging was poor, the carrier can be the most dangerous link in the chain. Some carriers will leave anything anywhere as long as the address matches. Some will not deliver mail to addresses if they think the person does not live there.

Think about it, would you take the time to send a package (letter mail is a little different) to someone and have the totally wrong shipping info. Shipping addresses are off by one digit or something all the time and if the carrier recognizes the name, will deliver to the person's correct address if they see a small error.

You can also get of the see through plastic sleeves and a card and list everybody who should receive mail at your address. I have one of those cards because at one point there were like 4 last names all at my house and the names were confusing the carrier. I recommend this as if the person is listed on the card, you can be pretty sure the carrier will deliver it.

I guess I went a bit off topic but bottom line is business + metro area + good vendor = safe
To person's name + house + poor vendor = not so safe, multiply by rural address and = even less safe

Just my opinion
