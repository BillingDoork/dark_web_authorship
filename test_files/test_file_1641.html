Title: Re: Advice about shipping to myself while staying at a hotel...
Post by: dontek on August 20, 2013, 12:34 pm
A few grams of coke?

Screw shipping it. Seal it up in 2 layers of mylar, trim them small. Then stick it in an envelope, put an address and return address on it and a stamp and throw it in your checked baggage. I would recommend getting some type of card or something and taping the "product" to the middle of the inside of the card and seal it up. Once that stamp goes on it with an address its considered property of the post office and falls under the same stipulations for search as does the mail that's in your mailbox.

Even if they absolutely were itching to, TSA isn't going to and cannot open that card without a postal inspector and a warrant. And they aren't going to spend all that time for something that small. Now if you're packing up bricks in your suitcase, thats a different story.

Just for the record . . .  although I have not verified this personally, don't get lulled to a false sense of security just because you have an stamped addressed sealed envelope & think that it is somehow a federal crime for anyone but the person it is addressed to to actually open it!

The reality of it is; that it has not yet been turned over to the federally protected system!  Until it is in the hands of the USPS, there are no protections . . .   I have seen mail that has been opened before getting to the post office by officers with no ramifications.  (It did not go to court with this situation though)

But it make no sense that there would be any protection until they had been in the possession of the feds first (USPS)

Title: Re: Advice about shipping to myself while staying at a hotel...
Post by: FIDBMG on September 12, 2013, 11:35 pm
It's really pretty easy. Call hotel, ask how your company or whoever should address mail to you while you're staying at the hotel. It's going to be Name, ATTN Guest Services, Hotel, Address.

Let them know you're expecting mail, and just to let you know when it arrives. If you're mailing it yourself, make it relevant to your stay. If you're getting stuff from a vendor, either just let it come or mail yourself an extra clean envelope with business stuff in it.

Tip $5 when you get there and $5 when it comes.
