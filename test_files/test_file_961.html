I lived in Japan for quite some time, with my wife who was a US service member. First off, YOU WILL GO TO PRISON if you're caught with drugs or importing drugs into Japan. To think otherwise is naive. Just google "military spouse pot japan". I just left Japan in December 2012, and I was already a member of SR, and even tried ordering 1/8th of pot, and spoke with the vendor and requested the tightest stealth he could manage, of course I paid extra, but I really wanted to smoke! Anyhow, I had my mailbox at the PO, and I also knew the combo(these boxes had combo locks that I've never seen before) to another box that was erroneously issued to me upon my arrival. So I was basically given a drop box, not linked to me. So, that being said, my order was seized by Japanese customs, and the only reason I wasnt incarcerated is because they thought the pack was intended for the true owner of the other box. The only way to really import via SR or another similar method, is to ship using Japanese carriers, and ship to Japanese addresses, with labels printed in Japanese. Trust me, it's the only way. Mail coming internationally to foreigners, particularly Americans/Europeans, is scrutinized much more thoroughly. I won't say how I know this, but please, if you want to stay free, take my advice. Their are alot of japanese express companies, you will see them all over delivering things from door to door. You must learn how to write your name and address in Japanese, THIS IS CRITICAL!!! You don't want the customs inspectors to be able to take one look at your package and know you're an American, or other foreigner.
Edit: As to how I knew my pack was seized, I performed some counter-surveillance from a safe distance each day around mail delivery time, and on the day i was expecting delivery, the police arrived with drug dogs to the PO.
Title: Re: Ordering to Japan
Post by: nanpa2001 on April 16, 2013, 04:23 pm
By the way, anyone that is ordering to a regular RESIDENTIAL address in Japan needs to know that Japan has a national system of registering residents to addresses. That means that if you send to a persons' house, the mailman has discretion on whether to deliver it or not.

For an office building or hotel, it doesn't matter.
