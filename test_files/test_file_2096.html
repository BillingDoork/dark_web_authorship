Title: Cannabis packaging technique - opaque inner bag?
Post by: farmer1 on August 24, 2012, 03:14 am
Does an inner opaque plastic layer make a package safer? This is assuming the cannabis is well sealed otherwise (smell proof) and the outer box is of solid condition.

I have had a couple buyers request some type of inner opaque packaging so that if the box was opened the cannabis itself would be hidden within an opaque layer. I have not personally heard of any problems without having an opaque layer, or had any myself. I was under the impression that if USPS had a warrant to open your package they are probably going to rip right through that opaque layer. As would your girlfriend, parents, neighbor, dog, etc. Is there something I am not aware of? What are the pros and cons of using another layer of opaque plastic?

I look forward to your comments. Security is of the utmost importance to me and I want to make sure that the best practices are put into use. I currently use plastic vacuum bags but am planning the switch to mylar with all the shipping I am now doing. That will make this a moot point but I am still interested in learning the why.
Title: Re: Cannabis packaging technique - opaque inner bag?
Post by: p0zen on August 24, 2012, 03:44 am
I think the concern of having the outer box punctured is real. Mishandling of USPS boxes is typical from my point of view, and if someone manages to put a finger-sized hole in the box ("somehow") a opaque layer between the buds and the box would be a security measure that would not be beneficial. I have seen some vendors have smell-proof baggies that have one side that is completely opaque (black). Mylar food bags are the best for nugs imo because they don't actually compress the nugs themselves, but that is just my opinion based on experience of buying from various vendors. But, even two pieces of cardboard do the trick, as long as no one can open it up/puncture it and obviously know its nugs.

Title: Re: Cannabis packaging technique - opaque inner bag?
Post by: StonerReakingHavoc on August 24, 2012, 08:55 pm
The reason people want a visual barrier is because if the package would accidentally get busted/ripped open then when they take the item out to put it into a new package or to try to fix the package it would be pretty obvious what it is and well there goes your buyers product and possibly their freedom.
On to the warrant statement, of course if they have a warrant they are going to rip it open. That's the whole point of a warrant...
I personally would not buy bud from a seller that won't put some type of visual barrier (some type of paper or cardboard etc..) either inside the vac seal or wrapped and taped around the vac sealed product.
Why? Because if it would get busted open and the postal worker doesn't know what it is because they can't see it, then they just package it back up and ship it on then you possibly saved my freedom and you will still have 1 more customer instead of 1 less one.
So it's a win win for both of us.
