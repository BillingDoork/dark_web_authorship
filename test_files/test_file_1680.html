Pretty much everything in the USPS has some kind of stamp on it. If you take a priority or express envelope up to the counter at a post office they charge you for the postage, and then put the correct stamp on it. They're not just for throwing things in blue boxes without going inside. I really don't see any employee noticing or caring about that method beyond whether or not the item has correct paid postage. I am sure there are other people doing the same thing and not shipping drugs, there must be.

And anytime I go into the post office I see all kinds of regular looking people shipping all kinds of things and boxes/mailers. Most are packaged very shoddily. So imo, if your illicit packages look markedly more professionally than the average, everything should be fine. The printed label, even weight distribution, no bulging, not excessively or oddly taped will all make the parcel seem like a legit package. Not something someone just threw together quickly to get out the door, which is what I see most individuals at the post office doing.
