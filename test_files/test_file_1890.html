The marks through the stamps?  Could have just been the postman manually cancelling the stamps so they cant be reused.  I think they usually have machines that do this but please dont quote me on that.  More likely even is that it was a different mailman than the one who regularly works your route, and this guy was being overly diligent and cancelled the stamps.

The x next to the return address is kind of weird though....My gut instinct is that the return address got run and came back as non-existent or flagged.  (Maybe the vendor had a package get "returned" to that address and the business reported it to the post office and police.  I don't know for sure but I would think that if it were the "x'ed out" return  address was flagged, they wouldn't have delivered your package, and definitely wouldn't have marked it so that you could see anything.  So most likely it was just that the address didn't exist...That's my best logical guess.  As far as the stamps go that's not unusual whatsoever bud.  Just their way of negating them and making sure they don't re-sent/reused.

Curious as to the marks on the other package but I don't think you have anything to worry about, especially if they actually delivered everything and you made it back on here to post about it.  That's always good news for sure.
