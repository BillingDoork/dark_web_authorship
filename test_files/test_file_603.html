Post by: thisworld on January 14, 2013, 07:50 pm
As people said before, it's likely just part of their automated system.  Most of the mail is sorted by machines and they check the barcodes(and place them) for their own purposes at the larger sorting facilities.

There's actually some really interesting information in one of the threads from earlier this year about how you really don't have to address a letter or package at all. you can merely imitate those small vertical lines you see near the bottom of domestic mail in the correct pattern and will arrive just fine.  You can also trick the machines into sending mail without any postage on it, but that's just begging for a felony that you'd be too ashamed to explain to anyone. haha
