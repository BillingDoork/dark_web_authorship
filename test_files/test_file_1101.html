Post by: tearghoa on May 13, 2013, 04:06 pm
This is really common for legitimate stuff. Call the hotel, tell them that you need to have a package sent for to you there during your stay and how do they want it addressed. Some hotels charge a fee to receive packages. Sometimes its at the front desk waiting for you or at the business center. The hotel business center will happily help you ship it back when you leave. Just pack it however you need, address it, and they will put metered postage on it for you. Send your packs by express mail so they arrive the same day as you check in.

Title: Re: How to send a package to my hotel?
Post by: paxpax on May 19, 2013, 03:46 am
This is common in the business world. Those who travel often and require materials from the office receive shipments all the time. They should be addressed as follows

Hotel Name
Receiver
Room Number
Street
City State Zip

Typically these shipments come from carriers other than usps, but usps would be treated the same. I would not recommend sending in anything other than a priority style letter mailer and use the return address of some business.

A day before the shipment is scheduled to casually ask the desk clerk if you have received a package, they of course would have not but now they know you are expecting something and will keep an eye out for it.

Routine stuff.
