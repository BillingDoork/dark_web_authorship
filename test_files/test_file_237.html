Title: PO box alternative: Bufferbox
Post by: Ktown99 on December 03, 2012, 12:58 am
Bufferbox could be an alternative to PO boxes.

They are currently being rolled out in the states and I think parts of Canada.

Anyone can open an account and from what I can tell ID is not required.

Buffer boxes are situated in GO stations, 7 elevens etc.

When you signup you are given a shipping address, send that address to the sender. You'll be sent an email from Bufferbox when the mail arrives, this will include a pin code to open the box.

You have 72 hours to pick up the parcel arrives.
