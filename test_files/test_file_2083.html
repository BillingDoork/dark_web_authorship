Quote from: The Mad Scientist on August 22, 2012, 12:05 pm

They are still in their original 'medical packaging' (strong plastic casing with push trough metal foil on the back).
You mean blister packs, yes?  The problem shipping these are the unavoidable  and unmistakable rattling noise they make.  The way to eliminate that is by poking a tiny needle size hole in each compartment followed by vac sealing.  The vac removes the air and forces the pills tight against the seal.

Title: Re: Shipping pills (Medical)
Post by: th3creeper on August 23, 2012, 08:21 pm
Lots of people prefer to receive pills in blisters because it is some measure of authenticity. Did you advertise them as such?  If not, you are much better off shipping them out of the blisters.  If you do ship them intact you want to deal with the rattling issue  as its impossible to hide that.  Here in the states if I didn't want to buy vac sealer but needed it for a one time use...I can go to most any retailer who carries them..make the purchase and then return it within 30 days for full refund.   If you plan on doing more selling its a worthwhile investment and not that pricey.

btw, if you ship without blisters and don't get a vac sealer I suggest you lay all the pills out flat on  table..then lay masking tape across them  ..flip and do same to other side so that the pills are sandwiched between 2 layers of tape.  Place your pill sandwich in between 2 pieces of heavy stock card and tape up.  This will prevent them from moving or being crushed during shipping. It also mks the pkg seem like it's only documents or photos.  You don't want to ship a pkg that is irregular or bunched up
