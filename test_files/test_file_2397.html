Just so everybody knows including you i'll explain how it works.

OP there's a few different ways each order can pan out everytime you make a purchase on SR. This is great for buyers to know as well.

When you place an order, the money you used to purchase said item goes into what we call escrow.
Escrow is essentially the 2nd to last step before the vendor can receive their money,
And is in place for your protection in the off chance your parcel doesn't arrive, a mutual resolution will be in place.. that will be discussed further down.

.. continuing on --

A vendor has 4 days in which your order can be held in processing.
If said vendor doesn't put your order in transit or cancel it within 4 days, your order auto cancels itself and your BTC returns back to you instantly.

Assuming he/she puts your order in transit you should, assuming you did research on the vendor, know how long shipping will take whether it's domestic or international.
This also of course depends on what shipping you purchase.

Normally what I do is wait 2-3 days after I was due to receive the package, while being in sync with when the vendor put your order in transit (**don't get this part twisted, start counting the shipping days AFTER the order gets put in transit**) before I message a vendor regarding ETA.
IE: If your order is priority shipping 2-3 day domestic, wait 5 days AFTER the order gets put in transit before messaging a vendor asking where your order may be.
This really helps vendors out if you follow a guideline similar to this. Vendors get a decent amount of messages asking where my order is from noobs within a few days of ordering.

In the event your package never comes, you will then instead of finalizing (assuming you didn't FE), will want to click the resolution button after XX amount of days.
The SR admins will then step in and from there they will ask the vendor for the DCN for your said parcel.
The vendor must then provide the delivery confirmation to an admin confirming that your package did or didn't arrive.
Depending on said circumstances, if the vendor can't provide the delivery confirmation the buyer will automatically win and get either a 50/50 or full refund, usually.
If the vendor DOES provide the delivery confirmation, well then you're most likely out of luck. Vendors copy your address EXACTLY how you write it (very rarely is it an address mistake).


Hope that cleared up any questions for you guys. I hope everybody follows that guideline! It really will put less of a strain on vendors like us :)
