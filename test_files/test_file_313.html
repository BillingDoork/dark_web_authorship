Title: Re: Shipping firearms US-UK
Post by: malacath on October 22, 2012, 10:52 pm
The problem with shipping guns is they are made of metal, and there are several structures in every gun that look basically the same, so an computerized x-ray machine can scan a lot of mail and pick out gun parts easily. I don't know how they x-ray mail, I would think they xray everything and have a computer pick out things that look like guns but maybe this is impractical, after all it is legal to ship guns if you are properly licensed so its not like every gun they find has to be illegal.

I would ship the gun in multiple shipments in pieces, this is a hassle and requires back and forth communication with the buyer, but its the safest way. Unlike shipping small quantities of drugs, if you get caught shipping a gun they will hunt you down. Ship one piece at a time and wait for the buyer to confirm that he/she got it before sending the next piece. You can probably disguise the barrel by shipping it with other metal rod like things, but keep the weight down.

I would do some more research on shipping guns, there are probably a lot of methods of doing so because I know guns are shipped illegally around the world all the time.
