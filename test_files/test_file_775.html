I don't mean to come off like I'm attacking you so please don't take it that way - but the truth is that you gave him an easy way to scam you. You should NEVER finalize early (unless you're a new buyer, in which case I would perhaps be slightly sympathetic towards the vendor). But other than that, if you do FE, you're unnecessarily putting yourself at risk.

My personal policy is that I never FE for anyone. If I see a vendor is asking for FE, I look elsewhere. The risk is just too great. You need to understand that when you FE, you are agreeing to take all the risk. Vendors don't like to admit this but the truth is that they risk very little by using the escrow system. They will have some BTC tied up in escrow, so what? And the small percentage of buyers who are scammers does not justify asking everyone to FE. So a vendor risks nothing by using escrow but you risk everything by FE'ing. Think about that.

If everyone agreed not to FE like I do, no vendor would dare ask for FE as they wouldn't get any business. But people persist in FE'ing and in the process continue giving vendors the power to demand early finalization.
