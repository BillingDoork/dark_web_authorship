My post doesn't apply if you live outside of the EU, cause I know jack shit about it.

I think it depends a lot on where you live and how much you order and from where you order. A small order domestic is *never* going to have a CD in most parts of Europe. They don't even check letters 99% of the time (in fact, in my country they aren't allowed to due to something called 'secrecy of correspondence', they can't x-ray letters), only packages. LE can't be bothered checking letters for a gram of something, it's simply not worth the effort for them. They go after suppliers.

Pretty sure as soon as it starts crossing countries (except EU, because EU there is no checking either) you are going to run into customs and chances are higher. Even then, letters will probably never result in a CD.

I think CDs will only happen on big international orders. If you order 200 or 500 pills from The Netherlands to the USA, then ye, you have a chance of getting a CD as the DEA flags you as a dealer.
