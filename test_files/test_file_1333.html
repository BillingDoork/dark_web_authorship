Title: Re: Using some guys mail box
Post by: sinnfein1488 on June 27, 2013, 09:09 pm
I guess alot depends on what you're having delivered, and other factors as well. One really unpredictable way to receive packs is to look up a UPS store/etc., contact them and ask them if they offer package acceptance services(they will say yes). Might cost $5. DO NOT GIVE THEM NAME/OTHER INFO. Place order using said address. Myself, I know EXACTLY when my packs will arrive, because i order from the same vendors and it ALWAYS comes in the same time frame. The day of delivery, just stroll in and grab your package, paying $5. This is really good if you think someone is on to you, but they only know of, or expect you to use a certain drop. Only you know where each one is going. Alternatively, open a VO box at similar store, and say you have a private business or the like, and send packs to "employees" or "colleagues" names. Most VO stores will gladly let you receive mail for others you deem necessary. If they say no, try the next one. It's fairly easy. I even have a PO box that isnt in my name. Just be resourceful. There's tons of good ways. But in my opinion, using some random person's house is just committing an unnecessary felony(removing mail from their box) to cover up the one you're already committing.

Post by: Abbie Hoffman on June 28, 2013, 02:05 am
Use a Mom & Pop store for a Private Mailing Box. I have a mid-size (~4" x 6") for $42/6 mo. All I needed was a fake DL and a fake auto insurance card.

I've rented a PMB from PostNet before and they didn't even ask for my ID when I applied! Really, just dress nice, play the businessperson-from-out-of-town role and you should find plenty of people willing to rent you your very own anonymous mailbox for minimal cost - cheaper than even a PO Box.

I would avoid renting a PO Box, as the USPS can be real bitches about forms and seem to be suspicious by nature. Go w/ the private sector, who loves your money and has no reason to be suspicious, unless they're holy-rolling, nosy busybodies. Avoid that kind.

I've done the borrow-a-mailbox thing before and it's a real pain. I was lucky to never get caught. Rural, isolated boxes, ideally out of sight from the owner's house, are best for this sub-optimal method. Timing can be everything and makes for a big waste of time often.

If you can find an isolated cluster of boxes, maybe you can get away w/ putting up your own box, complete w/ address lettering/numbers. Some mail carriers will deliver to anything with an address on it.

Just beware of getting caught doing something inadvisable like this, trying to avoid getting caught doing something easily deniable, like having bud mailed to your home.

Stay Safe.

Post by: thyme on June 28, 2013, 08:56 pm
What JKBliss said: The UPS Store can not open your USPS first class [which includes priority and express] mail without a warrant. The USPIS can not open your USPS mail without a warrant.
The UPS Store can (not acting as an agent of the law! just a concerned citizen!) certainly call LE to notify them of sketchy mail.
If you're picking up General Delivery parcels at the PO, expect to show ID.
Same for package acceptance services at a commercial mail receiving agency, and the ones I've been to make a copy of ID for the pick-up log.

Your UPS, FedEx, DHL, Mule Carrier Service, and other common carrier parcels can be opened by the carrier for any damn reason.

If you live somewhere that it is common to have packages sent Gen Del/package acceptance, maybe you'd blend in - this came up a lot in college towns, agriculture towns with migrant populations, etc.  Otherwise it's unusual... and you're trying to avoid standing out.

Problems with mail shipped to previous tenant.
It can be 1) forwarded, 2) noted as an irregularity and marked return to sender by the carrier [they do in fact get audited/checked on this stuff], 3) noted as an irregularity and therefore flagged for inspection. #1 and 2 being the most likely, but still not desired.
Lot of complaints/warrants out there that cite 'missing apartment number' as an irregularity. They're in the minority, but still, it's one more thing wrong.

So if there are 10 mailboxes, with people who know when their carrier comes, you really want to take the chance of getting caught? And this being the week the guy doesn't stay home sick?
My apartments have always been pretty... aware of the mailboxes, and with 10 apartments (for example), there's someone who's at home and comes down on time to check their mail.

Start a housesitting/dogwalking/errand business or something, perhaps? "I'll walk your dogs every day" [and, later,  offer to bring their packages in, or around to the back of the house. Starting with the mail offer would be creepy and weird.] Then be reliable and don't flake out once you get your pack. You'll have a) a reliable job, b) potentially a pool of addresses you can rotate, c) utter plausibility,  etc.
