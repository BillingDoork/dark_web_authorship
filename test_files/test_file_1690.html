Title: Re: Buying large amounts of envelopes and stamps?
Post by: MissNatural on August 31, 2013, 03:39 am
Buying envelopes at the post office won't raise flags, they sell them for a reason, but if you're going to be shipping out a lot of stuff, buy bulk from a shipping supplier, not the post office... or use the free flat-rate boxes. I can go to my post office a couple of times a week on differnet times during the day and get about 500 flat rate small boxes a week, for free. Just grab all of them in the cubby and walk out, totally legal and allowed. As far as stamps, I say buy as many as you want. They sell them for a reason. In any case, they would never try to bust you by asking the post office who buys the most stamps(face palm).

If you want to use your own envelopes, buy from a shipping supplier, or go to staples and get the padded packs of 10 for like $7. Slap on a priority sticker(which are free at the post office, you dont even go to the counter for them), an address label, and your stamps, and you're good to go.

I would also suggest you check out my thread in this shipping forum about printing your own tracking labels up. We're not sure if it works yet but the theory is sound and if it does work then every vendor will be able to have tracking without any connection to the tracking labels.

Title: Re: Buying large amounts of envelopes and stamps?
Post by: NW Nugz on September 09, 2013, 12:10 pm
Buying hundreds of dollars worth of $5+ stamps regularly can get you noticed. It may depend on the size of the town you are in. Many post offices don't even carry the big stamps and those that do may not have a lot of them out in the retail sales area. If you buy all they have a couple times, they may start to remember you. It is not illegal, but, I think it is best not to be noticed at a post office you might mail a larger package from. I say travel to a nearby town or... to buy large amounts of stamps or if you want a lot of Priority mailing boxes.
BTW, I think the Boxes in the lobby are usually flat-rate boxes.
If you use medium or larger size USPS boxes for light weight items, it can be a lot cheaper to NOT use Flat-rate postage. Order online if you can anonymously or ask at the counter for Priority boxes that are NOT flat rate boxes. They appear to still be free for Priority mailing. I think $7 is enough postage for any Priority package that weighs under a Pound.
Title: Re: Buying large amounts of envelopes and stamps?
Post by: RoxiPal on September 09, 2013, 08:39 pm
@NW Nugz is absolutely correct re: large stamp purchases and their availability at every single PO (some are too small to feel the need to stock them).

A few more words...: If you're in a large town/city, there is no need to increase your risk and ship packages from POs that you've stepped foot in before.  Assuming your paks are under 13oz, take advantage of the blue boxes and never step foot in a PO to mail packages.  If you must make PO drops, never overlap your stamp buying POs with your drop POs!

For the extra paranoid, I would suggest buying a random # of sheets at 'faraway' POs so that your purchases never begin to create a trail that follows a pattern.  This is, of course, assuming you (we) are (all) one man operations.  Good luck all
