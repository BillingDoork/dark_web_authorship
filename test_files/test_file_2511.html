Title: Re: How to get a tracking number without physically paying at the USPS counter?
Post by: killerbunnies on June 09, 2012, 09:04 pm
Go to an Automated Postal Center (APC) and you can use a prepaid debit card to purchase postage for USPS Flat Rate services. Find one preferably in a Post Office that does not have cameras. One thing to note the APC have a camera on the front of them below the screen but it is very easy to just lightly cover this. Dont cover it too much because if the camera recognizes there is no lighting the APC will say something like "Sorry, Im having technical difficulties right now try again later". With the Flat Rate services the cost of a tracking number or delivery confirmation number (DCN) is included. Then you can just stick the postage on the Flat Rate Envelope/Box purchased from the APC, along with one of the green delivery confirmation number stickers and you are set. Just drop off the package in a the drop box and you can track it online. :)
Title: Re: How to get a tracking number without physically paying at the USPS counter?
Post by: soyyo1114873 on June 12, 2012, 01:32 am
Better yet, just buy stamps and get some DCNs. Stick the DCN on the package and add extra stamps to cover the shipping + the cost of the DCN. You have to know the weight and the actual shipping rate, but any vendor is going to own a postal scale.

Presto! No worries about cameras, no debit card nonsense. Just drop in mailbox and that's it.
