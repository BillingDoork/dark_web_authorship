Title: Odd Shipping Question
Post by: chrisf on December 23, 2012, 04:57 pm
So I've been reading over quite a bit about the shipping aspect of SR and it seems that the general consensus is not to use a fake name, because that can raise suspicion with the post office who are not use to shipping that name to that address, and that you should NEVER ship to your home address. This makes a lot of sense to keep yourself safe from possible prosecution if the package is intercepted but I have found myself in an interesting situation and I'm wondering if I can break those rules. For the past three years or so my sister and I have both been receiving Amazon packages under a fake name just as a joke between her and I. Clearly this name is not registered to my home address and it seems to be clearly a fake name but all packages have arrived on time with no issue for about three years now. Do you think it would be safe to send a package from SR to my home address under this fake name because the postal service is use to seeing it arrive at my house? Do you think it may just be a fluke because the packages are in Amazon boxing? Should I just ship to a friends house? Thank you for any help you can offer!

Title: Re: Odd Shipping Question
Post by: gypsy on December 23, 2012, 08:14 pm
Quote from: chrisf on December 23, 2012, 04:57 pm
that you should NEVER ship to your home address.

Who told you this?  If you're in the US it's total BS.

Give your real name and your real address to all SR vendors.  If you read some more you will eventually find posts about plausible deniability. Anybody can send anything to anybody in the mail. Anything.  There's no stopping anybody who knows us from from sending us a packages that we didn't ask for.  If anything goes down just deny all knowledge.

Go ahead and use your real name & real address. If a package doesn't get delivered for a bad address and the vendor has a DCN you will be SOL with no refund & no recourse.  Do this enough times and you will likely end up blacklisted. Vendors have their own private forums. If has been posted here that vendors share information about bad buyers.

Hope this helps  :)
