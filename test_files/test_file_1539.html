While I'm not dismissing learning from documentaries..... Most documentaries about drugs, such as these "Drug's Inc" documentaries, cast a HUGE cloud of fear over everything. They make it sound like it's impossible to get away with, too easy to get caught, etc. Which in most cases such ideas couldn't be further from the truth(within reason). If these things are so hard to get away with, yet they make a documentary about how rampant and common of a problem that sort of stuff is, then that right there is telling you it's not as easy as they make it sound. A little inference goes a long way.

First of all, not everything gets X-rayed, it's most often suspicious packages that stick or have pill spilling out of them. Postal Inspectors, while it is their job to inspect things, most honestly could not give a rats ass about legitimate looking mail that may have drugs in it. It is too much of an inconvience for them to pull apart every peice of mail that could be something, because they would be pulling apart everything.

Postal inspectors do not pick up and analyze every package. They inspect the flow of mail. They have to do this for hours and hours looking at thousands of peices of mail so really only the extremely suspicious mail is going to stand out, stuff that's leaking liquid, stinking up the room, ticking/beeping(bomb/terrosim), etc.

Most controlled deliveries and seizurs happen due to poor/half assed packaging(or a snitch), which in any event it seems CD's are rare, at least here, because most vendors use some pretty good stealth.

It should also be noted Drug Dogs can only be trained to smell one drug. They don't have 10 dogs at every inspection center. From my experience and observation it seems the number one seized substance, judging by feedback, is Cocaine. Cocaine trafficking/importation is a HUGE problem for the DEA and is one of their main concerns. More cocaine is imported into the USA than any other drug, and it's probably similar for other countries. 1/2 of every coke orders(even small 1/2g orders) that I see on SR get lost/siezed.
