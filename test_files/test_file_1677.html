To begin with some information on how the Swedish Postal Service Operates can be seen in the thread Mail Systems around the World: http://dkn255hz262ypmii.onion/index.php?topic=172854.msg1240495#msg1240495

Quote from: ws
I am not a vendor on here but ship to Sweden quite a lot, from UK, as long as it has half decent stealth it will pass easy enough. Not sure what it would be like sending from outside of EU though.

Sweden and Australia most likely heavily profile Regions on how likely any drugs will arrive from there, so anything from example the Netherlands will have a much lower chance of making it.

What one has to keep in mind the Customs Agencies in Sweden are in one sense very good at what they do BUT they are also extremely inefficient in other regards. There is no such concept of a controlled delivery here in Sweden as if the package is small enough it "will" be delivered straight to the Mail Box ( they can however start a surveillance operation if it's enough product ). What will happen instead is the Customs Agency will seize the package and eventually send a modified "Love Letter" up to ten months later ( I know specifically of a case like this ) asking the person to come in and explain why they got sent drugs in the mail - depending on if they have enough evidence or not they will decide based on this if it's enough to prosecute or not ( it was not enough in this case I'm familiar with, also go figure ten months later is not exactly efficient work by them :P ). Edit: Forgot to add they will occasionally execute a search warrant without any warning during this ten month period if they feel it's warranted, this is also highly erratic and they've actually done it for amounts as small as 5 grams if they think the person ordering fits a special profile - ie. they think they can "save" him from taking drugs in the future.

Anyway my main point is the reason the Customs operate this efficiently is because of their extremely erratic nature in how they operate, probably not by design but that's just how it is. It's also kind of a Politics thing I'm suspecting as the Customs take their work A LOT more seriously than for example in Denmark. So if your packages can make it it's a good idea but there's a good reason Sweden/Australia have very high prices in general, and scamming is not one of the reasons at least for Sweden.
