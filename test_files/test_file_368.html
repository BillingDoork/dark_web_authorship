Title: Re: stop a package at the office?
Post by: opensky on December 10, 2012, 02:43 pm
I've been shipping legal products (not through silk road) for quite some time now and can offer you a bit of insight.

Usually when it says undeliverable, it means that you misplaced a few digits on the zip code your address is registered with at the postal office.

Meaning: the post office that has it is not the one that delivers it, so they have to re-ship it to the right one.

I've had this happen to me one time, package was supposed to come Friday, ended up being "undeliverable", I called, and they explained that it would show up Monday-Tuesday, it did.
