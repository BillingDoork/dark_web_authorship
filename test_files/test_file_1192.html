So now that TOR and SR has been gaining popularity in New Zealand we have been seeing a lot of new users joining the forum looking for information. With this has come the unwanted attention from hack news reporters who have nothing better to write on.

It's great that interest is increasing in New Zealand but it's time to start taking this a little more seriously. We're not 100% anonymous nor are we above the law so we need to take some steps in ensuring we all stay safe. The other threads pertaining to New Zealanders were closed and deleted at our request because discussion has recently become more flagrant with talk of where to buy from, which vendors to use, packaging and the likes.

The more we boast about it, the easier our mail becomes to profile and target and we would be stupid to think that law enforcement doesn't browse these forums (we already know reporters do and they don't paint a pretty picture). The goal of this thread is to keep us a little bit safer and therefore we have some basic rules that need to be followed

Rules

- No discussion on which vendors you should use. There are plenty of vendor review threads elsewhere on these forums and most worthwhile vendors will have relevant information on their SR page (if not just send them a message). If all else fails, I’d recommend sending a private message to other Kiwi users. More often than not we’re willing to help out and give recommendations.

- No discussion on the type of packaging a vendor uses. It puts the vendor and customers at risk as it becomes easier to profile their mail.

- Don’t come in here and ask why your mail from the Netherlands hasn’t arrived after two weeks. Mail from the Netherlands usually raises a flag no matter what country you’re in. They’re listed as a known drug producing country so my advice is just don’t bother. It might be a bit cheaper but it’s 99% guaranteed to be caught.

- Use the search function. If you have a general question regarding SR or bitcoins it has most likely already been answered elsewhere.

To the vendors; thanks for all your hard work. We really appreciate it. If you’re shipping to New Zealand, please keep us updated.
