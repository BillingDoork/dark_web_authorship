Title: USPS Priority Mail Delivery Map - New Tool
Post by: SmokesHisBroccoli on August 13, 2013, 12:31 am
So if you haven't checked out the usps.com site for awhile now's the time.  There have been some changes and most of them are small and minor.  USPS Express has a new name.  It's called USPS Priority mail express now.  It comes with $100 of insurance.  It's the same service that we know, but just a new name.

And then there's USPS Priority mail.  That's the 2-3 day service that most of us are used to.  What's cool is they now have something called "day specific delivery" which will tell you exactly how when a package should be delivered based off an interactive map you can find here (CLEARNET WARNING):

https://www.usps.com/priority-mail/map/

It's only as reliable as the USPS themselves obviously so take it with a grain of salt.  Never the less I like the tool and if you know your vendors state or general whereabouts because some of them do make that stuff known or you've dealt with them before you can have an idea of how long your package will take.
