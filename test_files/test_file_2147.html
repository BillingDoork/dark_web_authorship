The evidence is in the favour of the seller, unless he has been defrauding people lately. Always try to resolve issues by opening  dialogue with the vendor to see if you can work something out.

Title: Re: Mail says delivered...but, surely not to me!?
Post by: Regicide on August 30, 2012, 07:45 pm
The appropriate feedback score is a very tricky question for sure. anything below 5/5 is considered "negative" which is essentially flawed, because sometimes we have small issues that we feel don't deserve 5/5 but maybe a 4/5 if the packaging was bad but arrived.

In this case you have to use your judgement as to whether or not the vendor did his "due diligence" and shipped the product well packaged, and the package was not stolen because of poor packaging revealing its contents. If you feel the vendor did everything in his/her control to send the package then nothing below 5/5 is deserved.

Like Hamb said tho, if the vendor is a dick and accuses you of scamming when you're not, then a lesser score is appropriate. A truthful explaination of the occurance is always a good idea even if you get refudned. For example, if you write something like "Initial order was marketed 'delivered' but I did not personally receive it and it was likely not the fault of the seller. 50% refunded." This does not look negative to the buyer unless there are several people with the exact same issue, then perhaps it is the seller defrauding people or poorly packaging items.
