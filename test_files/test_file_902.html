Title: Re: Bad idea to get shipments from more than one vendor around the same time?
Post by: jmoney113 on March 30, 2013, 03:17 pm
I asked the same question before, and came to the conclusion that it's all relative. If you get a significant amount of mail usually, or enough that a few packages could blend in with daily mail, then I don't think you have a problem. If your mailman knows you don't get much stuff in the mail delivered, and all of a sudden have several parcels arriving at your address, then there might be some suspicion.

Many of the times I order I either have some other product coming as well like something I buy from Amazon, so that the rest of the packages blend in well and look legitimate. They usually coincide with delivery times, and I've had 4 packages come on the same day and everything was fine.

Title: Re: Bad idea to get shipments from more than one vendor around the same time?
Post by: newbottles on March 30, 2013, 05:01 pm
If they are all domestic I wouldn't think twice about it.

The best approach is to have a high volume of legitimate packages - even if a lot is UPS/FedEx.  Consider subscribing for grocery items at Amazon.  They will sometimes ship USPS as well which is ideal.

If you want to be really strategic, then order some sort of grocery item from another vendor every week that always ships USPS.  Or buy on eBay.  My guess is the vast majority of eBay sellers use USPS rather than private couriers.

Remember the fundamental idea is for SR packages to simply blend in with all the other packages in the system - and at your address.
