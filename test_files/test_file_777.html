Post by: th3creeper on March 01, 2013, 03:28 am
Quote from: johannsebastian on February 26, 2013, 03:53 am
Hey, I ordered an ounce of weed that was shipped last Wednesday morning and the DCN shows it as delivered today, but it definitely didn't come. Anyone have any advice on what I should do? Go to the post office with the DCN perhaps? Am I fucked?
Seeing as DCN no longer exists do you mean the tracking showed "delivered?"  Regardless...if it shows as being delivered then chances are it was in fact, delivered.  That's not to say it was delivered where it was supposed to be delivered.  Sometimes things get mistakenly delivered to the wrong address.  Another real possibility is that the shipping address was written or transcribed incorrectly and the pkg was delivered to the address written in error.  This happens quite often and rarely will the sender have any clear record of it.  The only real way I know for the sender to be able to show after the fact,  how they addressed a pkg is for them to either scan or photograph the pkg before sending out.  The PO won't be able to do anything for you once the carrier has scanned the tracking barcode as "delivered".

If it was addressed correctly but mistakenly delivered  to the wrong address there's a chance whomever received it would hand it back to the mail carrier who would then deliver it to you.  If that person happened to know you(like maybe it was delivered to a neighbor)you'd probably be in luck.  If it was put back into the system as a RTS(return to sender) it will get sent back to the return address on the pkg.  Of course, there's the chance whomever got your mail in their box(if that's what happened to your pkg) opened it and kept it or tossed it.

Your best chance to track it down on your own definitely would be to speak directly with the mail carrier who handled it and do it right away.  Chances are good they will remember the pkg and where they delivered it if it was that same day or yesterday.  However, ask them about it 2 or 3 days later and obviously your odds diminish.
