Title: Re: package sent to hotel?
Post by: semi_feral_human on July 19, 2013, 03:55 am
Personally wouldn't risk it for anything illegal or valuable.

It really depends on the hotel. It would probably be a good idea to talk to the front desk staff in person about receiving "a few urgent items off ebay" in advance, which might be difficult to impossible since you're only going to be there a couple days.

I had dozens of legal/grey area packages sent to me while staying in two different monthly motels. One mom and pop, one a chain. All went smoothly, except one package that got damaged and was spewing what the staff referred to as "sand" all over the place (poppy seeds from inside pods that broke during shipping).

There's always general delivery if it's a small town, but that has it's own level of sketch. This involves calling the post office and tell them you'll be in town camping / fishing / some plausible activity people would travel there to do. The mail goes to the PO and you show ID to pick it up. Address as follows: Dick Weiner
General Delivery
Anytown, TX 12345

So many things can go wrong with either of these options. Item could get lost and leave you in a strange town indefinitely to receive a trivial amount of drugs so the wrong person doesn't receive. Hotel staff could return to sender.
