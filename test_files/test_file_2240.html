Post by: chillychilly on July 06, 2012, 02:52 am
I have had many packages sent to Thailand but never anything dodgy.  I have had books mailed, sports equipment even a poster map of the world in one of those document tubes.

Every package was opened and I think the main reason for this is to check if any import tax is owed.  In Thailand there is import tax on virtually everything, even free stuff.

EG.  If someone sent you a demo of their band playing live, a cd or video, customs would determine a value for the cd, dvd or whatever and charge a tax rate based on their judged value.
hey
I started asking people I purchased books or tennis raquets off to put second hand or ex demonstration - and a much lower price.  On every occasion I did this they took my price as the price to work out tax from.

Knowing Thailand they took whichever was higher - lol  Seriously anything that saves time on something they can't make $ out of will be followed.  And they can't make $ out of a tax payment involving two people they don't know, so they want this part of the day over with quickly.

I did notice that if I used regular mail then I had to go to post office to pick it up, but if a courier such as FED EX, UPS, DHL was used they would come to my door with the package and a reciept for tax payment.  I had to pay cash to the delivery guy for the tax and I would recieve the package.

I'm guessing that the courier pay the tax to get package released, then claim the tax from the recipient.

This could be an interesting angle to look at as if the package if found to contain narcotics would they still pay the tax, cos they are almost certanly going to lose it...................
