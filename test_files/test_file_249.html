Post by: TheFireSwamp on December 05, 2012, 02:45 pm
HUGE piece of advice, I have just now, from this morning, had the police at my house and received a caution. Now I was lucky, the officer in charge had his head screwed on right.

My package got stopped by customs and they contacted my local police, etc, it was from Canada to the UK.

Now, I would have got away with it, totally fine, because I used a false name and denied any knowledge of the package or name. They went away.

What went WRONG was that I have a relative living with me, who unfortunately has a Cannabis related record from earlier in the year.

They came back within half an hour (after checking names of people living here, which again, was a MISTAKE, do NOT give names of anyone else living at your address if you suspect they have a record), unfortunately this was someone else telling them the names, not my own mistake.

They basically came back and started to arrest my nephew, which of course I couldn't let happen. But as mentioned I only got a caution, and the officer was very level headed and we both knew he was only doing his job (they left my bong and vaporizer for me and my medicine that was in my glass already cut up, so I had that waiting for me when I got home :).

So - you CAN deny knowledge, this will work.

IF you have someone living at your address with a previous cannabis charges, and they find the name and relate it to your address, they WILL be back.

I've had a very interesting experience with it all, did get a caution at the end of it, but not fussed, seeing as it was 1 oz and could've been a lot worse.

I know it's been said a few times - DENIAL
If they try arresting you for 'suspicion' - DENIAL
If you have a housemate with a past cannabis related offence, and they try arresting that person, make sure she/he understands - DENIAL
