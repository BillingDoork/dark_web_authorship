If your mail carrier's route would normally take him/her to your address before the guaranteed  delivery time then usually it will be delivered with your other mail.  If your mail usually comes late afternoon then of course, it will be delivered separately.  It's not unusual for carriers to deliver all Express mail for theirr usual route first thing..and then proceed on their regular mail route.  It wouldn't be unusual for you to see your regular carrier deliver to you twice in the same day if you had an Express pkg.  It's also not unusual to have non-postal Carriers(like a mail sorter or Supervisor, even) deliver express pks in their personal vehicles.  They'll pretty much do whatever they have to..to get Express mail delivered on time and they'll use whomever they need to to get it done.
