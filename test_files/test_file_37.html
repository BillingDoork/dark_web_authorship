Finland is a very small country. If someone uses their real address and gets caught, the customs office will look up the address for any information records, there include the phone number. Most of the cases there is one included, if there is not, they come knock your door.
Finland is also notorious for it's extremely strict customs, as only 5mil people are living here, there is not that much postal traffic from outside and they have enough resources to fully investigate most foreign packages.

Title: Re: possible BUSTED package to finland? ADVICE please
Post by: philo-juice on November 09, 2012, 12:19 am
I have also gotten phone calls from the customs. If they don't find your phone number, only then will they send a letter of seizure or come to your home door. All you gotta do is deny deny and deny and not know about anything. They cannot prosecute the first time though, but usually if you get caught the second time they can press charges, usually for personal amounts and small amounts its a fine ranging from 200€ to 1000€.

In Finland it's usually just two choices for a destination address, either an anonymous PO box or to your residence. The PO Box option is strongly not advised because post to these addresses are opened by the customs always, so your residental address is much safer, but as we have learned from this, it isn't bullet proof either.

The part on the opening of post is absolute 100% utter bullshit. While the post destined to a poste restante address goes through more security the people in the customs don't open the post without probable cause (K9 marking the letter/package, inconsistencies showing on xray etc.)

Safest way of ordering is using a real household address and denying everything if you get caught.
