Post by: Wazup7 on November 22, 2012, 01:59 am
I just want to point out the other side of this topic.  There are absolutely more anonymous ways to receive your SR goods, but...

If something illegal is delivered to a PO Box, it is very difficult to catch the receiver red handed, since police don't know when or if you will even pick it up.  They will have had to first identify that something was illegal, then they need to catch you in the act of receiving it.  The first part of that is rare enough--with reputable vendors, chances are close to 0.  Past that, I don't see how LEO hand-delivering your package to a physical location, where someone is expected to be present, is any less likely to be pursued, than putting it in your PO Box and waiting indefinitely for you to claim it.  Would they really deliver a box, and then post a police officer at the PO box location 24/7 until they can apprehend you?  It seems to me like PO Boxes offer slightly *more* protection than using a physical location, since you as the receiver can wait until 03:00 in the morning to get your package, rather than relying on your postal worker (or LEO) to deliver it to a location.

Don't misunderstand me, I know that the correct way to go is to deliver to a safe "dead drop", where you are not connected to the address, yet there is no risk of the postal service becoming suspicious (and ideally, no risk of anyone close to you being connected to the delivery).  I would recommend this route to everyone.  But for the other buyers who routinely have packages delivered to actual residences (yours or someone elses), is it really any safer than delivering to a PO box in any way?  I mean most of the issues surrounding delivery to a residence can be equally argued when considering delivery to a PO Box, no?

Am I severely misguided here?

What does it mean, "PO boxes automatically sign for you"?  I routinely have to sign for Express packages that get delivered to my PO Box, with the same information I have to sign for an Express package delivered to my office.  If they auto-signed, then that Express Packages wouldn't have been securely delivered, which is the entire point of the signature.

Are you proposing that a standard letter that gets put into a mailbox at your home is not "signed", but that the same letter put in your PO Box is "signed", even though it doesn't require any signature?

The signature *is not* meant to indicate that the party signing is taking posession of the contents--just of the package.  The signature is only meant to indicate that the package (with no regard to the contents), was delivered to the addressee.  If I have drugs shipped to my place of employment, would they really arrest the receptionist when she signs for it?  The idea of the postal signature is really pointless when discussing the legality surrounding "possession of the contents of a delivered parcel."

The terms of use for a USPS PO Box only vaguely indicate that illegal uses are not permitted, and the same provision is applicable to a residential address as for a PO Box.  They make no mention of signatures, presumabely because the same rules for signatures apply to PO boxes as any other address type, commercial or residential.
