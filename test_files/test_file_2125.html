Post by: Brave New World on August 28, 2012, 08:15 pm
I find it difficult to believe your university does not allow USPS to deliver mail to its receiving center.
If I were a mother looking to send a care package to my child at school I would choose USPS.
However, if that is truly the case, P.O. boxes do not require you to show your ID or talk to anyone when you go pick up your mail.
It is just like your mailbox.
However, you will have to show ID to open a P.O. box.
But like I said, you have nothing to hide. You are getting paper in an envelope so just use your real information.

The problem with P.O. Boxes is that there are a large number of vendors who are weary of mailing to P.O. boxes as they tend to be more scrutinized then residences.
If you do have to go the route of a P.O. box, make sure to get a big box so you never have to go to the counter if the package does not fit.
Get the biggest box they have.

Any more questions, just let me know.

First of all. I would advice against shipping to your university. Here is a rundown of how my university gets you your mail/packages.

Mail goes into a little mailbox you have a key to (quite safe I would think)
---------------
Packages on the other hand!
They go to a campus sorting facility where they email you a code that you need to pick up the package
upon picking it up you must SIGN AND DATE saying you picked up the package.
I have ordered a few packages (non-contraband) and they appeared to have been opened and smashed back together with half a roll of tape. I don't doubt that the university can and will go thru your packages.

This is just my experience and your university may be different, but I wouldn't even consider receiving a package here.
