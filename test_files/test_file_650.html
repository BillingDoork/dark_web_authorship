Title: Why is it always down to the customer?
Post by: fleetingglimpse on January 26, 2013, 07:32 pm
Why is it always down to the customer to prove non-receipt of a package? Iv had a couple of domestic, uk, deliveries no show recently. I had a bit of bother recently and wasn't able/didn't think to check on here to extend or resolve  the issue.

I will admit that some of the vendors were understanding when i explained my circumstances and agreed a 50% reship, and in some case a full reship!! Never received anything yet, but big up to them if i do! I'll even name them and thank them publicly if they don't mind.

However, i have been asked for a notice of seizure also, which i have never had! This is domestic UK remember. This implies two things to me- 1) The vendors packaging is so poor that a postie is able to identify a package and steal it for them self, or 2) The vendor never sent the package in the first place knowing that they will get some of, if not all of, the coin for nothing. Iv read through some of the feed back for the vendors and this seems to be a recurring theme for some, but not all i don't think, of them.

When i was asked for notice of seizure i asked for proof of postage, for which there was no answer. So think what you like.

I understand that vendors may not like this. Im not interested in causing arguments, but i just think that it is unfair to put all the emphasis on customers with decent purchasing stats to prove a negative, when it could easily be a selective scam.

Thoughts or discussion anyone?

fg
