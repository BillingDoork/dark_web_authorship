If you've EVER had ANY mail delivered to you at that address (even junk mail) ...

!!! USE YOUR REAL NAME !!!

Firstly, if you put no name at all it's likely to be return to sender by your postal carrier immediately.

Secondly, if you put someone elses name it will stick out like a sore thumb to your postal carrier, they have to sort like 300 houses or however many is on their route every single day of the week of mail.  After a while it becomes kinda auto-pilot for them to look for YOUR name on any mail because they have a shit tonne of mail to sort through before even delivering it, they do it every day, and they just learn to do it by memorization/pattern recognition to get their job done before the end of the day.

You want your illicit packages to BLEND IN and LOOK NORMAL.  When they see your name they throw it in the pile for your street.  They see someone else's name at that address they pick up that piece of mail and actually look at it to make sure they've read the right address.  Do you want that?  NO.

If you are worried about the police, they'll only catch you on a controlled delivery  They don't do on small mail and/or unless they've established a case against you, it's expensive and man-power consuming to carry out a CD, they're only going to set one up if they have some case to build on and it's large quatities/etc.

If pigs knock on your door for some reason and you have that piece of mail in your hand (and you havn't opened it) there is JACK SHIT they can do to you.  PLAUSIBLE DENIABILITY.

What if your crazy ex is sending you drugs to get you busted?  If you don't open it and are asked what it is you say "I HAVE NO IDEA, I NEVER ORDERED THIS AND HAVE NEVER HEARD OF THIS RETURN ADDRESS"

In other words, if you're THAT paranoid then just don't open the mail for a day or two, if no PO PO comes a knocking then you are fine.  Really though you should just open it (destroy the packaging) and put the illegal materials somewhere ya know ... hidden (off your property even).

If it's international and it's caught it will be seized, you'll be sent a letter saying it's been seized and to call XXX or go to XXX if you wish to claim it.  Burn the letter and DO NOT call or go to them, consider it a lucky loss.  Don't have illicit materials sent to that address again, however, if you get a love letter.

They're after the vendor's trying to import kilogram's of meth into the country, not $100 worth ... there just isn't enough man power for them to care and/or be able to bust every single small order of illegal materials.

Make everything look NORMAL and it will BE NORMAL.

Quote from: quinone on November 06, 2012, 11:13 pm
They don't do on small mail and/or unless they've established a case against you

Not true (at least in Australia). All it takes is one call from customs to the local authorities in your area, who then put the on-call detectives on the case, and snap. I have heard of people that have gone down this way, for such stupidly small amounts, all because they used their real address and real names.

If the address is in a rural location, or you live in a small town and you have no other means of sending it somewhere else, Id be more inclined to use your real name, as rural drivers always know each address and all the names associated with it. For somewhere like the suburbs/the city, Id always use a different name. Postmen are too busy to care about names in such places.
