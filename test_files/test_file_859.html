I watched a documentary several months ago about pill buyers who get their stuff sent to the US from other countries.  The most relevant part to your question came at the end of the documentary.  Mail bound for the US goes through international postal facilities where it is X-rayed for contraband.


From what I could see the process looked very much like baggage X-rays at the airport.  EVERYTHING in the package was revealed to the person doing the screening. Hiding your product in cardboard or other types of padding is useless. Pills in a blister pack, for example, were easy to spot. I don't know if every package gets sent through the X-ray machine but it looked like many of them did.

Post by: müslix on July 07, 2012, 10:41 am
I thought that Scandinavian customs are the "best". You will find a lot of bitching from Scandinavians about nothing getting through and no one shipping to them.

About the customs, that's pretty simple actually. Think like a customs officer, you are paid to find illegal or possibly harmful packages. The more and the bigger the hit the better (I think they actually get bonuses for big finds). Everyone who once had a summer job as mail man, has a small glimpse of what volume must go through customs every day. So what they try to do is filter all legit packages, so those they are searching actually contain something illegal and are worth the effort. They want that every packages they search contains something that's worth searching for. That's what they are paid for. The X-Ray is there to up the efficiency. Really nothing special here, they just optimize their workflow as best as they can to achieve their goal.
