Listen Friend...This is nothing more than the PO's standard operating procedure when a mail carrier has a pkg to deliver that requires a signature.  I have no idea how many people miss a signature required delivery each day in the states but it must be thousands if not tens of thousands of people.  There is absolutely nothing here that should cause you even a hint of worry. The best advice is for you to request re-delivery and be sure someone is there to sign.  You can't very well go to the PO to pick up .since you'll def need an ID.  Chances are the mail carrier is not going to ask for ID..he/she just needs an adult signature.  You can request re-delivery online from usps.com.

PS...It used to be they would automatically attempt a 2nd delivery with signature required mail and if needed a 3rd.  After that you had to go pick up within 10 days or it would be RTS.  The PO changed that policy a couple years back as a cost cutting measure.  It costs them money each time a piece of mail has to be handled.  That's why  it's not unusual for UPS and FedEx to try a 2nd delivery attempt on the same day if they missed someone the 1st time.  Once a pkg goes out for delivery they do everything they can to make sure it doesn't have to go back into the system.
