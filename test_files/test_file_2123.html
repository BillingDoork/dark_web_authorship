Title: Re: Is it bad to order from a lot of vendors at once?
Post by: Wazup7 on June 05, 2012, 02:00 am
If you can avoid it, then do that.  If you can't avoid it, try to at least ship it somewhere that isn't your house.

The fact is, when one package is sent to one address, that's one chance the feds have to profile, flag, and intercept your package.  When two packages are sent to one address from two different vendors, thats two chances the feds have to profile, flag and intercept.  Which is all okay, except if they intercept one package and find illegals in it, chances are, they will also go further and intercept the other packages going to the same desitnation address and find more illegals, thus adding to your troubles and giving them the extra evidence they were looking for..

As a general rule, use "dead drops" or locations that can't directly be lnked to you, and that would give you deniabilty in case the packages were in fact seized.  Also, try not to use the same address too often or for too long.  If you use PO Boxes, switch your PO number every few orders and try not to give the PO office your real address and name if you can avoid it.  The goal is that if the law showed up and accused you of ordering illegal stuff online, you should be able to say "no I didn't" and leave them nothing with which they can charge you.  It's just having 10 seperate packages arriving at the same location with your name on them will not go unnoticed once LE catches on.

Better safe than sorry, especially in this realm.

FWIW, I once had 3 orders coming to 3 addresses that were about 30 minutes apart.  they all ended up arriving on the same day, which kind of sucked driving-wise, but the point is if one of those orders was a controlled delivery, then LE would only have one piece of evidence, not 3, and one piece of evidence is much easier to defend than 3.
